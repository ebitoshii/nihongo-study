文ぶん法ぽう
Grammar Patterns

Key grammar patterns from N4 through N1, with explanations in English and Japanese, example sentences with furigana, and nuance notes.

N4
初しょ級きゅう
Elementary — foundational patterns
〜てあげるあげる／〜てもらうもらう／〜てくれるくれる
Giving and receiving actions
V-て + あげる／もらう／くれる

These three forms express the direction of a favour. あげる = I/someone does a favour for another. もらう = I receive a favour (done by someone). くれる = someone does a favour for me/my in-group.

友とも達だちに荷に物もつを持もってあげました。
I carried the luggage for my friend.
先せん生せいに説せつ明めいしてもらいました。
I had the teacher explain it to me.
⚑ Nuance
あげる can sound condescending if used about doing something for superiors — use てさしあげる for polite contexts, or rephrase entirely. くれる always has the speaker or their in-group as the recipient.
N4verbsgiving/receiving
〜ばよかったよかった
I wish I had… / Should have…
V-conditional (ば form) + よかった

Expresses regret about something that did not happen. The speaker wishes a past action had been taken differently.

もっと早はやく勉べん強きょうすればよかった。
I wish I had studied harder earlier.
⚑ Nuance
〜ておけばよかった (should have done in advance) and 〜なければよかった (wish I hadn't done) are closely related and worth learning together.
N4regretconditional
〜ためために
In order to… / For the purpose of…
V-dictionary / N + の + ために

Expresses purpose or reason. When used with a verb, it means "in order to do ~". When used with a noun, it means "for the sake of ~".

日に本ほん語ごを上じょう達たつさせるために、毎まい日にち練れん習しゅうしています。
I practise every day in order to improve my Japanese.
⚑ Nuance — ために (purpose) vs ように (purpose)
〜ために: purpose — used with volitional/intentional verbs. The subject consciously chooses the goal. 医者になるために勉強している (studying in order to become a doctor — deliberate goal).

〜ように: purpose — used with potential verbs, negative verbs, or stative verbs. The goal is a state or ability rather than a direct action. 日本語が話せるように練習している (practising so that I can speak Japanese). Also used for requests/instructions: 忘れないようにメモしてください.

Key rule: ために + volitional verb; ように + potential/negative/stative.
N4purpose
〜そうだ（伝でん聞ぶん）
I heard that… / They say that…
Plain form + そうだ

Hearsay そうだ reports information heard from another source. It attaches to the plain form of verbs and adjectives.

明あす日たは雨あめが降ふるそうです。
I heard it is going to rain tomorrow.
⚑ Nuance
Critical distinction: Hearsay そうだ (plain form + そうだ) vs Appearance そうだ (V-stem or adj-stem + そうだ). 「雨あめが降ふりそうだ」= It looks like it will rain (appearance). 「雨あめが降ふるそうだ」= I heard it will rain (hearsay).
N4hearsayreported speech
〜ておく
To do in advance / to leave in a state deliberately
V-て + おく

Indicates doing something ahead of time for a future purpose, or intentionally leaving something in a certain state. Implies forethought and preparation.

旅行りょこうの前まえに予約よやくしておきました。
I made a reservation in advance of the trip.
窓まどを開あけておいてください。
Please leave the window open.
⚑ Nuance
Colloquially contracted to 〜とく: 「買かっとく」= I'll buy it (in advance). Common in everyday speech.
N4preparationて-form
〜てしまう
To end up doing… / to do completely (often with regret)
V-て + しまう

Two uses: (1) completion with finality — the action is entirely done; (2) regret or unintended consequence — something happened that the speaker didn't plan or want.

財布さいふを忘わすれてしまった。
I went and forgot my wallet (unfortunately).
宿題しゅくだいを全部ぜんぶ終おわらせてしまった。
I finished all my homework (completely done).
⚑ Nuance
Colloquially contracted to 〜ちゃう (て verbs) and 〜じゃう (で verbs): 「忘わすれちゃった」、「飲のんじゃった」.
N4completionregretて-form
〜ていく／〜てくる
To go doing / to come doing; continuation away from or toward speaker
V-て + いく　／　V-て + くる

〜ていく: action/change moves away from the present point (into the future or away from speaker). 〜てくる: action/change moves toward the present point (from the past, or toward speaker).

気温きおんがどんどん上あがっていく。
The temperature keeps rising (going forward).
だんだん寒さむくなってきた。
It has gradually become cold (change reaching now).
⚑ Nuance
Also used literally: 「走はしっていった」= ran away; 「走はしってきた」= came running. The temporal/directional metaphor extends from the physical meaning.
N4directionchange over timeて-form
〜てみる
To try doing something
V-て + みる

Expresses trying or attempting an action to see what happens, often out of curiosity or experimentation.

この料理りょうりを食たべてみてください。
Please try eating this dish.
一度いちど日本にほんに行いってみたい。
I want to try going to Japan once.
⚑ Nuance
〜てみる implies uncertainty of outcome — do it and see. It does NOT mean "try hard" (that is 〜ようとする). Think of みる as "see" — "do and see what happens".
N4attemptて-form
〜てはいけない
Must not… / Not allowed to…
V-て + はいけない

Expresses prohibition — the speaker tells someone not to do something. Stronger than 〜ないでください; closer to a rule or firm instruction.

ここで写真しゃしんを撮とってはいけません。
You must not take photos here.
⚑ Nuance
Casual contractions: 〜ちゃいけない／〜じゃいけない. Compare with 〜てもいい (permission) — exact opposites in structure: 「食たべてもいい」vs「食たべてはいけない」.
N4prohibitionて-form
〜てもいい
It's okay to… / May… (permission)
V-て + もいい

Grants permission or expresses that something is acceptable. Also used to ask for permission: 〜てもいいですか？

窓まどを開あけてもいいですか。
May I open the window?
⚑ Nuance
〜なくてもいい = "don't have to" (no need to do). NOT the same as prohibition. 「来こなくてもいい」= You don't have to come (it's fine if you don't).
N4permissionて-form
〜なければならない
Must… / Have to… (obligation)
V-ない stem + なければならない

Expresses obligation or necessity. One of the most important N4 patterns. The speaker (or subject) is compelled to do something.

明日あしたまでにレポートを提出ていしゅつしなければならない。
I must submit the report by tomorrow.
⚑ Nuance
Contracted in speech: 〜なきゃ (very casual), 〜なければなりません (formal). Also 〜なくてはならない／〜なくてはいけない — all express obligation. 〜なきゃ is the most common spoken form.
N4obligationない-form
〜なくてもいい
Don't have to… / No need to…
V-ない stem + くてもいい

Expresses that something is not necessary — the subject is free to skip it. The opposite of 〜なければならない.

今日きょうは急いそがなくてもいいですよ。
You don't have to hurry today.
⚑ Nuance
Do NOT confuse with 〜てはいけない (must not = prohibition). 「来こなくてもいい」= don't have to come. 「来きてはいけない」= must not come. Very different!
N4no obligationない-form
〜ないで
Without doing… / Please don't…
V-ない form + で

Two uses: (1) without doing X, do Y (manner); (2) soft request not to do something.

朝食ちょうしょくを食たべないで学校がっこうに行いった。
I went to school without eating breakfast.
心配しんぱいしないでください。
Please don't worry.
⚑ Nuance
Compare 〜ずに (more formal/literary equivalent of "without doing"): 「食たべずに行いった」. 〜ないで is the natural spoken form; 〜ずに is used in writing and appears at N3.
N4negationmannerない-form
〜ないといけない
Have to… / Must… (casual obligation)
V-ない form + といけない

A colloquial way to express obligation, commonly used in everyday speech. Functionally equivalent to 〜なければならない but less formal.

薬くすりを飲のまないといけない。
I have to take my medicine.
⚑ Nuance
Register ladder: 〜なければならない (formal/written) → 〜なくてはいけない (neutral) → 〜ないといけない (casual spoken) → 〜なきゃ (very casual). All mean the same thing.
N4obligationcolloquial
〜ことができる
Can… / To be able to…
V-dictionary + ことができる

Expresses ability or possibility. More formal than the potential verb form (e.g. 食べられる), but functionally equivalent.

私わたしは漢字かんじを読よむことができます。
I am able to read kanji.
⚑ Nuance
ことができる is preferred in formal writing and JLPT tests. In conversation, the potential form is more natural: 「読よめる」. Both are correct and interchangeable in meaning.
N4abilitypotential
〜ことにする
To decide to… (speaker's deliberate decision)
V-dictionary / V-ない + ことにする

The speaker makes a deliberate, volitional decision. They choose to act (or not act).

毎日まいにち運動うんどうすることにした。
I decided to exercise every day.
お酒さけを飲のまないことにしました。
I decided not to drink alcohol.
⚑ Nuance
Contrast: 〜ことにする (active personal decision) vs 〜ことになる (outcome decided by external forces). 「東京とうきょうに転勤てんきんすることになった」= It was decided (by the company) that I transfer to Tokyo.
N4decisionvolition
〜ことになる
It has been decided that… / It turns out that… (external decision)
V-dictionary / V-ない + ことになる

Indicates a decision or outcome that results from external circumstances rather than the speaker's personal choice.

来月らいげつから大阪おおさかに住すむことになりました。
It has been decided that I will live in Osaka from next month.
⚑ Nuance
〜ことになっている = "it is the rule/custom that…" or "it is supposed to be…": 「ここでは禁煙きんえんすることになっている」= Smoking is not allowed here (that is the rule).
N4decisionexternal circumstance
〜ようにする
To make an effort to… / to try to make it so that…
V-dictionary / V-ない + ようにする

Expresses an ongoing effort or habit the speaker is trying to maintain — less of a one-time decision than ことにする; more of a continuous endeavour.

野菜やさいを毎日まいにち食たべるようにしている。
I am making an effort to eat vegetables every day.
遅刻ちこくしないようにしてください。
Please try not to be late.
⚑ Nuance
ようにする (effort/habit) vs ことにする (one-time decision). ようにする implies you are not fully there yet — you are working toward the goal.
N4efforthabit
〜ようになる
To come to… / to reach the point where… (gradual change)
V-dictionary / V-potential + ようになる

Describes a gradual change in ability, habit, or state over time. Particularly used with potential verbs to express newly acquired ability.

練習れんしゅうして、泳およげるようになった。
I practised and became able to swim.
最近さいきん、早はやく起おきるようになった。
Lately I've come to wake up early.
⚑ Nuance
Key nuance: gradual change — you weren't able to before, now you are. 〜なくなった is the negative counterpart: "came to no longer do / be able to".
N4change of stateability
〜と（条件じょうけん）
If / When… → automatic/natural result
Plain form + と

Expresses a natural, automatic, or inevitable result. If A, then B always follows. Used for facts, instructions, directions, and natural cause-effect.

このボタンを押おすと、電気でんきがつく。
When you press this button, the light turns on.
右みぎに曲まがると、駅えきが見みえます。
If you turn right, you will see the station.
⚑ Nuance — Four Conditionals Compared
〜と: natural/automatic result. Cannot be used with requests, suggestions, or volitional endings in the result clause. 春になると、花が咲く.

〜たら: most versatile — sequential "once/after X then Y", or hypothetical. Works with volitional endings. 家に帰ったら、電話して.

〜ば: focus on the condition being sufficient. Often used with advice. お金があれば、買えるのに.

〜なら: based on assumed or stated context. 日本へ行くなら、京都へも行って. The condition is often already known/stated.
N4conditionalautomatic result
〜たら（条件じょうけん）
If / When… (after condition is completed) — most versatile conditional
V-た form + ら

The most versatile conditional. "If/when X is completed, then Y." Used for hypothetical situations, future conditions, and past discovery ("when I did X, I found…").

宿題しゅくだいが終おわったら、遊あそんでもいいよ。
Once your homework is finished, you can play.
家うちに帰かえったら、電話でんわしてください。
When you get home, please call me.
⚑ Nuance
Unlike 〜と, 〜たら CAN be followed by requests and commands. Also used for counterfactuals: 「もっとお金かねがあったら…」= If I had more money… (but I don't).
N4conditionalversatile
〜ば（条件じょうけん）
If… (hypothetical/general condition)
V-eba form / i-adj → ければ / na-adj/N → であれば

Expresses a general or hypothetical condition. "If A, then B (will/should happen)." Often used for advice, rules, and hypotheticals.

毎日まいにち練習れんしゅうすれば、上手じょうずになれる。
If you practise every day, you can become good at it.
⚑ Nuance
〜ば is commonly paired with いい to give advice: 「どうすればいいですか」= What should I do? Mastering all four conditionals (と・たら・ば・なら) is a key N4 milestone.
N4conditionalhypothetical
〜なら（条件じょうけん）
If that's the case… / If it's true that… (context-reactive)
Plain form / N + なら

Used when the speaker responds to or assumes something the listener has said or implied. Condition B is based on accepting A as given.

日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうするなら、この教科書きょうかしょがいい。
If you're going to study Japanese, this textbook is good.
⚑ Nuance
〜なら is the only conditional where the result can logically precede the condition: 「行いくなら、今いま出でなさい」= If you're going, leave now. It reacts to context, not a sequence of events.
N4conditionalcontext-reactive
〜そうだ（様子ようす）
Looks like… / Seems like… (from visual evidence)
V-stem / i-adj stem (drop い) / na-adj + そうだ

Expresses an impression or inference based on direct sensory observation. The speaker can see or sense that something appears a certain way.

この料理りょうりはおいしそうだ。
This dish looks delicious.
雨あめが降ふりそうだ。
It looks like it's going to rain.
⚑ Nuance — そうだ (appearance) vs そうだ (hearsay) vs らしい vs ようだ
〜そうだ (appearance): based on direct visual evidence right now. 雨が降りそうだ (it looks like it's going to rain — I can see dark clouds).

〜そうだ (hearsay): reporting what you heard. 明日は雨だそうだ (I heard it will rain tomorrow).

〜らしい: inference from indirect evidence or hearsay, less certain. 彼は病気らしい (he seems to be sick — I heard/noticed signs).

〜ようだ／みたいだ: inference from direct observation, speaker's conclusion. 彼は病気のようだ (he seems sick — based on what I see). みたいだ is more casual.
N4appearanceinference
〜らしい
It seems that… / Apparently… (indirect evidence)
Plain form + らしい

Expresses an inference based on indirect evidence, hearsay, or general knowledge. The speaker is drawing a conclusion from available information.

あの映画えいがはおもしろいらしい。
That film apparently is interesting.
⚑ Nuance
らしい also functions as a suffix meaning "typical of / befitting": 「男おとこらしい」= manly. This is a separate adjectival use — context makes it clear which meaning is intended.
N4inferenceindirect evidence
〜ようだ／みたいだ
It seems / It appears / Looks like (resemblance or inference)
Plain form + ようだ　／　Plain form + みたいだ

Both express appearance or resemblance. ようだ is formal/written; みたいだ is casual/spoken. Both can mean (1) it seems like / (2) is like (comparison).

彼かれは疲つかれているようだ。
He seems to be tired.
まるで夢ゆめみたいだ。
It's just like a dream.
⚑ Nuance
ようだ attaches to: plain form verbs / な-adj + な / N + の. みたいだ attaches directly without な or の. In JLPT writing, use ようだ; in speaking practice, みたいだ is natural.
N4appearanceresemblance
〜と思おもう
I think that… / I believe that…
Plain form + と思う

Quotes one's own thoughts or opinions. Attaches to the plain form of verbs and adjectives. Essential for expressing opinions politely.

明日あしたは晴はれると思おもいます。
I think it will be sunny tomorrow.
⚑ Nuance
と思っている = currently hold the belief (ongoing). と思った = thought at a moment in the past. For third person: 「田中たなかさんはいいと思おもっている」= Tanaka-san thinks it's good (ongoing).
N4opinionquotation
〜と言いう
To say that… / called… / known as…
Plain form + という / N + という + N

Used for direct or indirect quotation ("said that…"), for naming things ("called X"), and referencing ("something called X").

彼女かのじょは「行いかない」と言いった。
She said "I'm not going."
「さくら」という喫茶店きっさてんに行いきましょう。
Let's go to a café called "Sakura".
⚑ Nuance
というのは is used to define or clarify: 「自由じゆうというのは難むずかしい」= "Freedom" is a difficult concept. This nominalising use is very common in explanations.
N4quotationnaming
〜こと（名詞化めいしか）
The fact of / the act of (nominaliser)
V-dictionary / V-ない + こと

Nominalises a verb clause so it functions as a noun. Used after verbs of liking, knowing, beginning, etc.

泳およぐことが好すきです。
I like swimming (the act of swimming).
彼かれが来くることを知しっていた。
I knew that he was coming.
⚑ Nuance
こと vs の as nominalisers: の is more colloquial and experiential; こと is for abstract facts. 「彼かれが来くるのを見みた」(saw him come — witnessed) vs 「来くることを知しっていた」(knew he was coming — factual knowledge).
N4nominalisationgrammar core
〜くなるなる／〜になるなる
To become… (change of state)
i-adj → く + なる　／　na-adj / N + に + なる

Expresses a change into a new state. i-adjectives use く form; na-adjectives and nouns use に.

外そとが暗くらくなってきた。
It has been getting dark outside.
日本語にほんごが上手じょうずになりたい。
I want to become good at Japanese.
⚑ Nuance
Pair with 〜くする／〜にする (transitive — to make something a state): 「部屋へやをきれいにする」= to make the room clean. なる = change happens naturally; する = someone causes the change.
N4change of stateadjectives
〜すぎる
Too much / excessively…
V-stem / i-adj stem (drop い) / na-adj + すぎる

Indicates excess — too much of something, to a degree that is problematic or beyond what is appropriate.

昨日きのう、食たべすぎてしまった。
I ate too much yesterday.
この映画えいがは長ながすぎる。
This film is too long.
⚑ Nuance
すぎる conjugates as a regular る-verb. Noun form: すぎ: 「食たべすぎ」= overeating. Common in warnings and self-deprecating remarks.
N4excessdegree
〜やすい／〜にくい
Easy to… / Hard to…
V-stem + やすい　／　V-stem + にくい

Attaches to the verb stem to express ease or difficulty of performing an action. Both behave as i-adjectives.

この本ほんは読よみやすい。
This book is easy to read.
この字じは読よみにくい。
This handwriting is hard to read.
⚑ Nuance
These refer to the inherent nature of the object, not personal ability. 「読よみにくい」(hard to read — the text is difficult) vs 「読よめない」(can't read — my inability). A subtlety JLPT tests frequently.
N4easedifficulty
〜たい
Want to… (first person desire)
V-stem + たい

Expresses the speaker's own desire to do something. Conjugates as an i-adjective. Cannot be used directly for third-person desires.

将来しょうらい、日本にほんに住すみたいです。
In the future I want to live in Japan.
⚑ Nuance
For third person use 〜たがっている: 「弟おとうとはゲームをやりたがっている」= My brother wants to play games (visible desire). Using たい for third person implies mind-reading — generally avoided.
N4desirefirst person
〜つもりだ
Intend to… / Plan to…
V-dictionary / V-ない + つもりだ

Expresses a firm personal intention or plan. Stronger than と思う (just thinking about it) — the speaker has already decided.

来年らいねん、JLPT N1を受うけるつもりです。
I intend to take the JLPT N1 next year.
⚑ Nuance
〜たつもり (past + つもり) = "under the impression that / acting as if": 「説明せつめいしたつもりだったが…」= I thought I had explained it, but… A subtle and very natural usage.
N4intentionplan
〜から／〜ので
Because… / Since… (reason/cause)
Plain form + から　／　Plain form (+ な for na-adj/N) + ので

から: subjective reason, used for requests and personal justifications. ので: more objective cause-effect; sounds politer in many contexts.

雨あめが降ふっているから、出でかけたくない。
Because it's raining, I don't want to go out.
体調たいちょうが悪わるいので、早退そうたいさせてください。
As I'm feeling unwell, please let me leave early.
⚑ Nuance — から vs ので
〜から is subjective — it expresses the speaker's personal reason or justification. It sounds direct and can be assertive. 〜ので is objective — it presents a reason as a natural, factual cause. It sounds more polite and softer. In formal writing and polite requests, ので is strongly preferred. から can sound blunt or even rude in formal contexts.

Compare: 眠いから帰ります (I'm leaving because I'm sleepy — direct, personal) vs 眠いので帰ります (I'm leaving as I'm sleepy — softer, more polite).
N4reasoncause
〜のに
Even though… / Despite the fact that… (unexpected/disappointed)
Plain form + のに

Expresses contradiction or disappointment — the result is contrary to what was expected. Carries an emotional charge: frustration, surprise, or regret.

あんなに勉強べんきょうしたのに、試験しけんに落おちた。
Even though I studied so hard, I failed the exam.
⚑ Nuance — のに vs けど／が
〜のに always carries emotional weight — surprise, frustration, or regret at an unexpected result. It cannot be used neutrally. 〜けど／が simply connect contrasting clauses without emotion.

Compare: 頑張ったのに、失敗した (Despite trying hard, I failed — frustration) vs 頑張ったけど、失敗した (I tried hard, but failed — neutral).

Cannot use のに for future plans: ✗ 明日は雨なのに、行きます → ○ 明日は雨だけど、行きます.
N4concessiondisappointment
〜し
And also… / What's more… (listing multiple reasons)
Plain form + し (repeatable: A し, B し, → C)

Lists multiple reasons or qualities, implying there are even more. Often used to justify a conclusion or express cumulative reasoning.

この店みせは安やすいし、おいしいし、また来こたい。
This restaurant is cheap, the food is good — I want to come again.
⚑ Nuance
し implies the list is non-exhaustive — there are additional reasons not mentioned. Contrast with て-form (sequence) and も (additive). し specifically lists reasons behind a judgement or feeling.
N4listing reasonscumulative
〜られる（受身うけみ）
To be done (to) — passive voice
ru-verb: V-ない stem + られる　／　u-verb: V-ない stem + れる

The passive form. The subject receives the action. Two types: (1) direct passive; (2) adversarial passive — subject is inconvenienced by someone else's action.

私わたしは先生せんせいに褒ほめられた。
I was praised by the teacher.
雨あめに降ふられて、困こまった。
The rain came down on me and I was stuck. (adversarial passive)
⚑ Nuance
Conjugation: 食べる → 食べられる / 書く → 書かれる / 飲む → 飲まれる / 話す → 話される. Irregular: する → される, くる → こられる. The adversarial passive is uniquely Japanese — it has no clean English equivalent.
N4passivegrammar core
〜させる（使役しえき）
To make someone do / to let someone do
ru-verb: V-ない stem + させる　／　u-verb: V-ない stem + せる

The causative form. The subject causes or allows someone to do something. Can mean compulsion ("make") or permission ("let"), depending on context.

先生せんせいは生徒せいとに作文さくぶんを書かかせた。
The teacher made the students write essays.
子供こどもに好すきなことをさせてあげた。
I let the child do what they liked.
⚑ Nuance
Causative + てください = polite request to be allowed to do something: 「説明せつめいさせてください」= Please let me explain. Extremely common in business and formal situations.
N4causativegrammar core
〜させられる（使役受身しえきうけみ）
To be made to do (against one's will)
V-causative form + られる

The causative-passive — the speaker is compelled to do something by someone else, and generally does not want to.

上司じょうしに残業ざんぎょうさせられた。
I was made to work overtime by my boss.
⚑ Nuance
U-verb short form common in speech: 書かせられる → 書かされる; 飲ませられる → 飲まされる (u-verbs only, not ru-verbs). Mastering this form marks solid N4 grammar competence.

〜ながら
While doing… / simultaneously doing A and B
V-stem + ながら

Indicates two actions happening simultaneously by the same subject. The main action is in the main clause; the secondary (background) action is marked with ながら.

音楽おんがくを聴ききながら、勉強べんきょうする。
I study while listening to music.
⚑ Nuance — ながら vs まま vs つつ
〜ながら: doing two actions simultaneously. Both actions are in progress. 音楽を聴きながら勉強する (study while listening to music).

〜まま: a state left as-is, unchanged. 靴を履いたまま入った (entered with shoes still on). The focus is on the unchanged condition.

〜つつ: formal/literary equivalent of ながら, often with an underlying contradiction. 分かっていつつ、やってしまった (knowing better, I still did it). Also used in つつある for ongoing change.
N4simultaneous actions
〜まま
As it is / leaving something in its current state
V-た / V-ない + まま　／　adj + まま　／　N + の + まま

Indicates that a situation or state continues unchanged, and a subsequent action occurs in that unchanged state.

靴くつを履はいたまま、家うちに入はいってしまった。
I entered the house with my shoes still on.
電気でんきをつけたまま寝ねてしまった。
I fell asleep with the lights still on.
⚑ Nuance
V-ないまま = in the state of not having done: 「宿題しゅくだいをしないまま学校がっこうに行いった」= went to school without having done homework. まま carries a slight nuance of carelessness.
N4state continuation
〜とき
When… / At the time of…
V-plain / adj + とき　／　N + の + とき

Marks a point or period in time when something happens. The tense of the verb before とき matters and changes meaning significantly.

子供こどものとき、よく川かわで泳およいだ。
When I was a child, I often swam in the river.
日本にほんに来きたとき、桜さくらが咲さいていた。
When I came to Japan, the cherry blossoms were blooming.
⚑ Nuance
Tense contrast: 「日本にほんに来くるとき」(while on the way / before arriving) vs 「日本にほんに来きたとき」(upon arriving / after arriving). This is a classic JLPT N4 test point.
N4timetemporal clause
〜前まえに／〜後あとで
Before doing… / After doing…
V-dictionary + 前に　／　V-た + 後で

Sequence markers. 前に uses the dictionary form regardless of overall tense. 後で uses the past (た) form because the first action must be completed.

寝ねる前まえに、歯はを磨みがいてください。
Please brush your teeth before sleeping.
食事しょくじの後あとで、散歩さんぽしました。
After eating, I went for a walk.
⚑ Nuance
Key asymmetry: 前に always takes dictionary form (the action hasn't happened yet). 後で always takes た-form (action is complete). This is a guaranteed JLPT N4 grammar question topic.
N4sequencetemporal
〜間あいだに
While / During (a window in which something else occurs)
V-ている / N + の + 間に

Indicates that a second action occurs within the time window of the first. The secondary action happens at some point during (not throughout) the first action.

彼女かのじょが寝ねている間あいだに、料理りょうりを作つくった。
While she was sleeping, I made dinner.
⚑ Nuance
間に vs ながら: 間に = two actions by different people or actions at a point within a window; ながら = same person, truly simultaneous. 「テレビを見みながら食たべる」vs「テレビを見みている間あいだに電話でんわが来きた」.
N4temporal windowwhile
〜でしょう／だろう

Plain form + でしょう (polite) ／ だろう (plain)

Expresses conjecture or probability based on reasoning. でしょう is polite; だろう is plain/casual.

明日あしたは寒さむいでしょう。
It will probably be cold tomorrow.
⚑ Nuance
Rising intonation on でしょう？ turns it into a tag question: 「そうでしょう？」= Right? / Don't you think? This tag-question usage is extremely common in conversation.
N4conjectureprobability
〜かもしれない
Might… / Maybe… / Perhaps… (~50% or less certainty)
Plain form + かもしれない

Expresses possibility with lower certainty than でしょう. The speaker is uncertain — there is a chance, but it's not definite.

鍵かぎを忘わすれてきたかもしれない。
I might have left my keys behind.
⚑ Nuance
Certainty scale: かもしれない (~30–50%) → でしょう (~70%) → はずだ (logical expectation) → にちがいない (N3: near certainty). Placement on this scale is a key grammar concept.
N4possibilityuncertainty
〜はずだ
Should be… / Supposed to be… (logical expectation)
Plain form + はずだ　／　N + の + はずだ

Expresses a strong expectation based on logic, knowledge, or prior information. The speaker is confident something is true based on reasoning.

彼女かのじょは今頃いまごろ、着ついているはずだ。
She should have arrived by now.
⚑ Nuance — はずだ vs にちがいない vs かもしれない vs でしょう
〜はずだ: logical expectation based on known facts. High confidence, reason-based. 彼は来るはずだ (he should come — I know he said he would).

〜にちがいない: strong personal conviction, near-certainty. 彼が犯人にちがいない (it must be him — I'm convinced).

〜かもしれない: possibility, 50% or less. 雨が降るかもしれない (it might rain).

〜でしょう／だろう: conjecture, seeking agreement, or softening assertion. 明日は晴れるでしょう (it will probably be fine tomorrow).

Confidence order: にちがいない ＞ はずだ ＞ でしょう ＞ かもしれない.
N4expectationlogical certainty
〜について
About… / Concerning… / Regarding…
N + について

Marks the topic of discussion, study, or thought. Functions as a postposition meaning "about X / regarding X".

日本にほんの文化ぶんかについて勉強べんきょうしています。
I am studying about Japanese culture.
⚑ Nuance — について vs に対して vs に関して
〜について: general "about/concerning" — broad topic. 日本の文化について話す (talk about Japanese culture).

〜に対して: directed toward — actions, attitudes, or contrasts aimed at someone/something. 批判に対して反論する (counter the criticism directed at us). Also contrasts two things.

〜に関して: more formal version of について — "regarding/with respect to." Used in official contexts. この件に関してご説明します (I will explain regarding this matter).

Rule of thumb: に関して ≈ について but more formal; に対して implies direction/reaction/contrast.
N4topic markerpostposition
〜によって
Depending on… / By means of… / Due to… (agent / variation / means)
N + によって

A versatile postposition with three main uses: (1) agent in passive sentences; (2) means or method; (3) variation depending on circumstances.

人ひとによって意見いけんが違ちがう。
Opinions differ depending on the person.

⚑ Nuance
In passive sentences, に marks an animate personal agent; によって marks agents more formally or abstractly (organisations, historical figures, inanimate causes).

〜ても

V-て + も　／　adj-て + も　／　N/na-adj + でも

Expresses a concessive condition: regardless of whether A happens or is true, B holds. "Even if A, still B."

雨あめが降ふっても、行いきます。

どんなに〜ても / いくら〜ても = "no matter how much…" — emphasises that even an extreme degree of A won't change B. Very common in passionate or determined statements.

〜だけ
Only… / Just… / As much as…
N / V-plain / adj + だけ

Limits the scope to the stated amount or extent. Can mean restriction ("only") or sufficiency ("as much as possible / as much as one wants").

Please come as quickly as possible.
⚑ Nuance — だけ vs しか〜ない vs のみ vs ばかり
〜だけ: "only/just" — neutral limitation. 一つだけ食べた (I ate only one).

〜しか〜ない: "only" with a negative feeling — insufficiency or limitation. 百円しかない (I only have 100 yen — not enough). Must use negative verb.

〜のみ: formal/written equivalent of だけ. 関係者のみ入場可 (entry for staff only).

〜ばかり: "nothing but, always" — often implies excess or criticism. ゲームばかりしている (always playing games — excessive).

〜しか〜ない
Only… (with negative verb — implies insufficiency)

Emphasises that only X exists or remains, often with a nuance of "that's all there is" — suggesting it is not enough. Always used with a negative verb.

財布さいふに500円えんしかない。
I only have 500 yen in my wallet (and that's all).
⚑ Nuance
だけ vs しか: 「500円えんだけある」= There are only 500 yen (neutral). 「500円えんしかない」= There are ONLY 500 yen (negative nuance — not enough). しか carries emotional weight of lack.
N4limitationonlynegative

N + ほど　／　V-plain + ほど　／　〜ば〜ほど

Indicates degree or extent, or sets up a proportional comparison. 〜ば〜ほど is the classic "the more A, the more B" structure.

死しぬほど眠ねむい。

The more you practise, the better you become.
⚑ Nuance
ほど also marks negative comparisons: 「東京とうきょうほど大おおきくない」= Not as big as Tokyo. This is different from より (positive comparison) — ほど only appears in negative comparative sentences.

Pre-intermediate — expanding expression
〜わけだ

Plain form + わけだ

Expresses a logical conclusion that naturally follows from a given situation. The speaker draws an inference from known facts — "so that explains it."

10年間ねんかん日本にほんに住すんでいたんですね。日本語にほんごが上手じょうずなわけだ。
You lived in Japan for 10 years? No wonder your Japanese is so good.
⚑ Nuance — わけだ family compared
〜わけだ: "so that's why / it makes sense that." Used when you understand the reason for something. 三年間勉強したわけだ、上手なはずだ (so you studied 3 years — no wonder you're good).

〜わけではない: "it's not that..." — partial negation. 嫌いなわけではない (it's not that I dislike it).

〜わけがない: "there's no way that..." — strong denial. そんなことができるわけがない (there's no way that's possible).

〜わけにはいかない: "cannot (due to social/moral obligation)." 約束があるから、行かないわけにはいかない (I have a commitment, so I can't not go).
N3logical conclusion
〜ものの
Although… / Even though… (but result is unexpected)
Plain form + ものの

Concessive conjunction. Acknowledges a fact but introduces a contrasting or disappointing result. More formal and written than けれど.

日本語にほんごを勉強べんきょうしたものの、なかなか話はなせるようにならない。
Although I studied Japanese, I still can't seem to speak it well.
⚑ Nuance — ものの vs のに vs が／けど
〜ものの: "although" — acknowledges a fact but presents a contrasting, often disappointing result. 合格したものの、喜べなかった (although I passed, I couldn't be happy). More formal.

〜のに: "even though" — strong emotion (frustration/surprise) at an unexpected result. 頑張ったのに失敗した (I worked hard but failed — frustrated).

〜が／けど: simple contrast, neutral. No emotional weight. 高いが、買った (it was expensive but I bought it).

Rule: ものの = formal concession; のに = emotional contrast; が／けど = neutral contrast.
N3concessivecontrastformal
〜さえ〜ば

N + さえ + V-conditional (ば)

Expresses that a single condition is the only thing needed for a result. Emphasises minimality — just this one thing is enough.

If only I had time, I could practise more.
⚑ Nuance
さえ alone means "even" (surprising inclusion): 「子供こどもさえ知しっている」= Even children know it. The さえ〜ば construction is specifically the conditional minimality pattern.

〜くせに

Plain form + くせに　／　N + の + くせに

Concessive conjunction with a strongly critical or reproachful nuance. The speaker feels the subject should know better or act differently given their situation.

Even though they knew, they didn't tell me. (how irritating)
⚑ Nuance

N3concessivereproach

Plain form / N + にもかかわらず

Formal concessive: despite condition A, result B still occurred. More written/formal than のに or ても.

⚑ Nuance
Primarily written and formal; common in news reports, academic writing, and speeches. The casual spoken equivalents are のに or ても. かかわらず comes from 関わる (to be involved/concerned).
N3concessiveformal
〜ほうがいい

V-た / V-dictionary + ほうがいい

Used to give advice or make a recommendation. V-た (past form) is more commonly used for affirmative advice; V-ない is used for negative advice.

早はやく寝ねたほうがいいですよ。

You'd better not go there.
⚑ Nuance
For affirmative: V-た + ほうがいい sounds more natural and decisive than V-dict + ほうがいい. For negative advice, always use V-ない (not V-なかった). This tense asymmetry is a common test point.

〜より〜のほうが

A より B のほうが + adjective

The standard comparative structure. B is being compared favourably against A. より marks the "less" side; ほうが marks the "more" side.

電車でんしゃより飛行機ひこうきのほうが速はやい。
Aeroplanes are faster than trains.
⚑ Nuance
より can stand alone as "than": 「前まえより上手じょうずになった」= Got better than before. In writing, より also means "from" (e.g. in formal letters): 「〇〇より」= From ○○.
N3comparison

Not as … as … (negative comparison)

A is not to the same degree as B. B sets the upper benchmark; A falls short of it.

The film wasn't as interesting as I expected.
⚑ Nuance
ほど in this structure always pairs with a negative predicate. The positive comparison uses より: 「B より A のほうが〜」. These two patterns are the core of Japanese comparative grammar.
N3comparisonnegative
〜ため（に）

Plain form (cause) / V-dict / N + の + ため（に）

At N3, ために is used for cause/reason as well as purpose. With a past/state verb it reads as cause; with a dictionary-form verb it reads as purpose. Formal register.

Distinguish: V-dict + ために = purpose ("in order to"); N/past verb + ために = cause/reason ("because of"). The same form, different meaning based on what precedes it — context and tense are the clues.
N3causepurposeformal

Cites the source of information. "According to X, …". The main clause typically ends with そうだ, らしい, とのことだ etc.

〜おかげで: because of (positive result). Gratitude implied. 先生のおかげで合格できた (thanks to the teacher, I passed).

⚑ Nuance — にほかならない vs に過ぎない vs に違いない
〜にほかならない: "is nothing other than, is precisely" — strong affirmative identification. これは失敗にほかならない (this is nothing other than failure). Formal, emphatic.

〜に過ぎない: "is nothing more than, merely" — minimising or limiting. これは始まりに過ぎない (this is merely a beginning). Often used to deflect excessive praise or to limit a claim.

〜にちがいない: "must be, no doubt" — strong certainty based on reasoning. 彼が犯人にちがいない (he must be the culprit).

Tone: にほかならない = emphatic positive; に過ぎない = minimising; にちがいない = certain inference.

〜にあたって
On the occasion of… / When undertaking… (formal preparatory)
V-dictionary / N + にあたって

Marks a significant occasion or undertaking upon which the speaker takes specific action or makes a statement. More formal than とき; used for major events and milestones.

卒業そつぎょうにあたって、先生せんせいに感謝かんしゃの気持きもちを伝つたえた。
On the occasion of graduation, I conveyed my gratitude to my teacher.
⚑ Nuance
にあたって is used for one-time significant occasions; に際して is a close formal synonym. Compare に先立って (prior to — preparatory) vs にあたって (on the occasion of — concurrent with the event). にあたり is the more formal written variant.
N1occasionformal
〜に際さいして
On the occasion of… / At the time of… (formal)
V-dictionary / N + に際して / に際し

Formal marker of a significant occasion or juncture, at which a specific action is taken. Very close in meaning to にあたって but slightly more formal and tends to appear in written announcements.

契約けいやくに際さいして、内容ないようをよく確認かくにんしてください。
At the time of signing the contract, please confirm the contents carefully.
⚑ Nuance
に際して ≒ にあたって in most contexts. に際し is the written contracted form. These appear in business letters, legal documents, and official guidelines. Not used for everyday events — reserved for significant transactions or milestones.

〜をおいて
Other than… / Apart from… / There is no one but… (exclusivity)
N + をおいて（ほかに）〜ない

Asserts that nothing or no one other than X is qualified or suitable. Used with a negative result clause to express that X is the only option.

この仕事しごとができるのは、あなたをおいてほかにいない。
There is no one other than you who can do this job.
⚑ Nuance
をおいて ほかに〜ない is the fixed pattern. It is a strong, formal expression of uniqueness or irreplaceability — a high compliment or exclusive claim. Compare にほかならない (asserting identity) vs をおいて (asserting uniqueness/irreplaceability).
N1exclusivityuniquenessformal
〜といえども
Even though… / Even if one says… / Even (status) cannot…
Plain form / N + といえども

Concessive: even granting/acknowledging the stated condition, the result still holds. Formal and literary — stronger than ても and implies an extreme or prestigious case being conceded.

専門家せんもんかといえども、すべてを知しっているわけではない。
Even an expert does not know everything.
⚑ Nuance
といえども is literary and formal. It often introduces a prestigious, extreme, or seemingly invincible case and then concedes its limitation. Compare といっても (casual: "although it's called…") — same root but very different register.
N1concessiveliteraryextreme case
〜きらいがある
Has a tendency to… / Is somewhat inclined to… (negative tendency)
V-dictionary / N + の + きらいがある

Expresses a slight but notable tendency toward something undesirable. More restrained and formal than 〜がちだ (tends to do — N3); carries a critical but measured nuance.

彼かれは物事ものごとを大げさおおげさに言いうきらいがある。
He has a tendency to exaggerate things.
⚑ Nuance
きらい here is NOT the adjective 嫌い (dislike) — it is a classical noun meaning "tendency/inclination." Always used for negative tendencies. More formal and written than 〜がちだ; common in essays, literary criticism, and evaluative writing.
N1tendencynegativeliterary
〜ともなると
When it comes to being… / Once one reaches the level of…
N + ともなると / ともなれば

Marks a notable status, stage, or scale, stating that at that level naturally certain things follow. The implication is that the level is impressive enough to have distinctive consequences.

社長しゃちょうともなると、責任せきにんも大おおきい。
When it comes to being a company president, the responsibility is great.
⚑ Nuance
ともなると implies the level is high or significant enough that certain outcomes are natural or expected. The subject is usually a position, age, scale, or occasion. Compare になると (when it becomes — more neutral, no implied impressiveness).
